THERE ARE MANY BENEFITS TO PARTICIPATION FOR BOTH CONSUMERS AND ORGANIZATIONS

The benefits to the participating consumers

  • Empowerment-a sense of ownership of projects;
  • Gaining awareness of organizational decision making and funding;
  • Gaining practical skills and knowledge;
  • Building self-esteem and self-awareness;
  • Gaining confidence in voicing their own opinion;
  • Improved participant and staff relationship, as participants have a better understanding of how the project works and the limitations of resources;
  • Moving towards self-help;
  • and receiving services which are responsive to their needs.

The benefits for the nonprofit

  • Ensures that consumers are the starting point for services, which are based on research and consultation with consumers rather than making assumption about needs;
  • Breaks down barriers between staff and consumers;
  • Improves the understanding of need between staff and consumers;
  • Improves the understanding of need between staff and consumers;
  • Staff feel satisfied that they are delivering what consumers want;
  • Consumers provide their ‘insider’ knowledge to improve the organization’s services;
  • The sense of ownership means there’s less likely to be incidents of damage or vandalism; and
  • and Re-addresses the power balance by including those previously excluded.
Only the person wearing the shoes knows where they pinch In 2012, I organized a series of community meetings on homelessness. In a small room sat twelve members of the public eager to learn and eager to share their views.
A man in his late 60’s chimed in to say that he knew that “the homeless” were all “shiftless, drug abusing, users of the system” and knew that if they wanted housing it was there for them. What this gentleman did not expect was that several individuals in this small group were homeless.
A woman in her fifties turned to him and gasped. She then shared her story, one that included a high paying civilian job with the Navy, breakdown of a family, an unexpected illness and an admission of pride that prevented her from seeking the help she needed before she became homeless. Her parting words to the group and pointedly to this gentleman was “only the person wearing the shoes knows where they pinch”.
Her wisdom and this gentleman’s ignorance are key reasons why this course exists. Non-profits can only know about the needs and pain points of consumers by asking and involving them.
