INTRODUCTION

This course is intended for non-profits, community based organizations, faith-based groups or any group interested in:

  • Increasing the participation of consumers in your organization.
  • Undertaking consultation activities such as surveys, focus groups, and interviews with consumers.
  • Involving consumers in the everyday running of the organization such as on boards, and committees.

Where on the ladder is your organization?

Key Definitions

Consumers-Consumers are the users of your programs and/or services.

Participation- The basic principle of participation is to consult with and actively include consumers in the decision-making process. It is a process of involvement which empowers individuals and creates projects and services based on need rather than assumptions. Individuals are not seen as passive recipients of a service, instead they are active participants

Participation can be broken down into three categories.

1. Consultation

Engagement that is a one-time occurrence, one-off.

Example

2. Involvement

Engagement that is on-going, but may be time limited.

Example

3. Integration

Integration differs from involvement. With integration consumers are typically a critical part of the everyday operating of the organization, involved in the policies, programs etc.

Example

Why participation?

Nonprofits need to have clear reasons as to why they have a participative approach. This ensures all stakeholders have a good understanding of its purpose and the principles behind it.

For example, participation may be part of a funding requirement, can influence customer care, support and encourage empowerment.

Funding requirements

Participation is increasingly being included in statutory funding requirements. In some funding criteria, proposals are required to provide evidence that participation has in influenced the development of the proposal. Ongoing funded is also dependent on whether a nonprofit meets targets for consumer participation. A nonprofit that embraces a participative approach will ensure they meet funder requirements.
