MEANINGFUL PARTICIPATION OR TOKENISTIC?

Case Example #1 REVISITED-Tokenistic Approach

A large non-profit, providing emergency shelter to persons who are homeless is required to involve consumers on their board and to have regular on-going consumer involvement. Their funder expects that consumer’s voices will help to inform and improve the way services are provided.

To address their funder's requirement the shelter hands out surveys once a year. The questions are yes and no questions related to their satisfaction with the shelter. There is no other option to add any other feedback. The shelter does nothing with the surveys other than collect them and does not provide survey participants any feedback after the survey. To fulfil the requirement to have a consumer on the board, the shelter staff recruit a consumer but do not involve him in any discussions or provide any assistance for him to attend the board meetings.
