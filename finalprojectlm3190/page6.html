LESSON SUMMARY

Where do you want your organization to be with participation?

IN SUMMARY

In summary, how do non-profits practically engage consumers in a meaningful way? For many organizations, it starts with how you view your consumers. If you view consumers as passive recipients of services, then you will struggle to involve consumers, meaningfully.

However, I genuinely believe that most people who work in non-profits believe that consumers have both the right and the capacity to be involved. Where we can struggle is the practicalities – 'there’s no time, we don’t have the money, there’s nobody to do it, nothing will change anyway and we can’t get consumers interested'.

On occasion, providers, planners and workers cop out of consumer participation and regurgitate the hallowed excuses--but it is important to note that capacity, time, money and expertise are genuine issues.

Accordingly, three priorities in terms of making participation really work are:

  • Statements and policies which reflect a commitment to meaningful participation,
  • a board that is supportive and holds everyone accountable to ensuring meaningful participation, and
  • genuine consideration of the value and contribution that consumer participation brings to the organization. One that helps the organization to grow, develop and expand based on consumer input (the people wearing the shoes).
